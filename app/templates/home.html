District Court Faridabad

The city of Faridabad was founded in the year 1607 by Shaikh Farid, also known as Baba Farid. He was an eminent Sufi saint and the treasurer of Mughal emperor Jahangir. The city was established to protect the Grand Trunk Road and was later announced as the headquarters of Faridabad Pargana under the rule of Ballabgarh.

Tewatia Jat Sardar Gopal Singh attacked the city after Aurangzeb's death. He established a treaty with Murtija Khan, the Mughal officer of Faridabad, and became the Chaudhary of the Pargana. After the Mughal rule, the British took over.

In 1950, after the partition of India, there was a large influx of refugees from Pakistan. Industries were established to rehabilitate them, and people from different communities, regions, and religions settled in Faridabad.

Initially part of the Gurgaon district, Faridabad was established as a separate district on 15th August 1979. Today, the city is home to numerous large-scale industries, including manufacturing and IT sectors. District Courts, Faridabad came into existence on 08.12.1980 with Shri A.P. Chaudhary as the first District Judge.
